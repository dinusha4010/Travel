Welcome to Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka, located in South Asia, is an island nation known for its rich cultural heritage, diverse landscapes, and welcoming locals. From pristine beaches and lush tea plantations to ancient temples and wildlife reserves, Sri Lanka offers a captivating blend of history, nature, and vibrant traditions. Travelers can explore UNESCO World Heritage sites, savor flavorful cuisine, and enjoy warm hospitality in this gem of the Indian Ocean.

Top 8 Places to visit in Sri Lanka

sigiriya

Sigiriya or Sinhagiri is an ancient rock fortress located in the northern Matale District.Sigiriya was designated as a world heritage site in 1982.

Sigiriya is famous for it's palace ruins on top of a massive 200 meter high rock surrounded by the remains of an extensive network of gardens, reservoirs and other structures

Kandy

Kandy is a major city in Sri Lanka located in the Central Province. It was the last capital of the ancient kings' era of Sri Lanka. 'Dalada Maligawa', Temple of the Tooth Relic is located in the Kingdom of Kandy, which houses the relic of the tooth of the Lord Buddha.Known as a pilgrimage destination for the Temple of the Tooth Relic, a listed UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Yala

Yala was designated as a wildlife sanctuary in 1900, along with Wilpattu, designated in 1938, as the first two designated national parks in Sri Lanka.

Nine arch

The Nine Arch Bridge, also known as the 'Bridge in the Sky' was constructed by connecting two bog mountains when constructing the Badulla.

Galle Fort

Set along the coast and made up of crooked alleyways, the tropical Galle Fort is a melting pot of past and present influences.

Pollonaruwa

The ancient city of Polonnaruvo has a history of more than 1,500 years. This is a UNESCO world heritage list and is worth visiting.

Sri Lankan Food

Main Dishes

Breakfast

Apart from the breads and the dahl,a Sri Lankan breakfast will almost certainly include another type of curry,either potato,chicken or sometimes even fish curry.

Lunch

Lunch cuisine is boiled or steamed rice, served with a curry of fish or meat, along with other curries made with vegetables, lentils, or fruits Dishes are accompanied by pickled fruits or vegetables, chutneys, and sambols.

Dinner

many Sri Lankan dishes are considered by many to be among the hottest in the world in terms of chilli content.

Spices

Sri Lanka Spices

Abba - Mustard. Black mustard seed is very pungent and acrid.
Enasal - Cardamom. An exotic addition to rice dishes and confectionary, especially in the Sri Lankan national pudding, Watalappam.
Curundu - Cinnamon.
Sera - Lemon Grass.
Sadikka - Nutmeg.
Velliche Misis - Chilies.
